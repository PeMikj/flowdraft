

"This project introduces a Categorical Flow Map (CFM) drafter into the Orthrus lossless parallel-decoding framework. Orthrus accelerates a frozen autoregressive (AR) LLM by attaching a trainable diffusion head that drafts a block of K tokens in parallel over a shared KV cache, then has the frozen AR head verify them, guaranteeing the output matches the base model exactly. Its speedup is capped by acceptance length, and its drafter is a single-step masked-diffusion model whose conditional-independence proposal limits how many tokens get accepted.
We replace that drafter with a few-step categorical flow map that produces a more correlated, higher-fidelity joint proposal over the block in one (or few) jumps. The novel technical core is a dual distillation objective: the flow-map drafter is distilled from the frozen AR teacher (matching its per-position distribution like Orthrus) while preserving flow-map consistency (endpoint/ECLD self-distillation like Categorical Flow Maps). The hypothesis is that the flow-map drafter raises acceptance length, and therefore throughput, at equal verification cost, with strictly lossless output. "   "1. Reproduce the Orthrus baseline. Frozen AR backbone + single-step masked-diffusion drafting head, shared KV cache, block-causal mask (causal to context, bidirectional within block), and the lossless inference loop (parallel projection -> AR verification with greedy consensus / rejection sampling). Tractable scale: a 0.5B AR model, drafter-only training.
2. Build the Categorical Flow Map drafter. Replace the masked-diffusion head with a simplex flow-map head (endpoint parametrization) that drafts the K-token block in 1-few jumps conditioned on the shared AR KV cache; argmax/sample to produce discrete candidates for verification.
3. Develop the dual distillation objective. Train the flow-map drafter (frozen backbone) with a combined loss: (a) an AR-teacher distillation term aligning the drafter's per-position endpoint distribution to the frozen AR head, and (b) a flow-map consistency term (ECLD / endpoint self-distillation) enabling accurate single-jump drafting. Stage the training (teacher-matching first, then consistency) to balance the terms.
4. Plug into lossless inference unchanged. Verify that the flow-map drafts route through the frozen AR verifier with bit-exact equivalence to the base model, across greedy (T=0) and sampling (T>0).
5. Evaluate and compare. Benchmark acceptance length, Tokens-Per-Forward-Pass (TPF), and wall-clock throughput for: (i) AR baseline, (ii) reproduced masked-diffusion Orthrus, (iii) flow-map drafter (1 jump), (iv) flow-map drafter (few jumps). Confirm losslessness in every case; sweep block size K and number of jumps to map the acceptance-vs-passes frontier; ablate the two distillation terms."
